# Sha'ar ha-Kavanot — Source Catalog

## Primary Source
- **Title:** שער הכוונות (Sha'ar ha-Kavanot / The Gate of Intentions)
- **Author:** Rabbi Chayyim Vital (1543–1620), recording the teachings of Rabbi Isaac Luria (1534–1572)
- **Edition used:** Koretz 1784
- **Digital source:** [hebrewbooks.org/14347](https://hebrewbooks.org/14347)
- **Copyright status:** Public domain

## About the Work
The Sha'ar ha-Kavanot is the fourth of Vital's Eight Gates (Shemonah She'arim). It is the most detailed manual of theurgic prayer in the Lurianic tradition — a gate-by-gate instruction for aligning human consciousness with the divine structure during the fixed cycles of Jewish liturgy.

## Structure (19 Derushim across 4 volumes)

| Vol | Derushim | Scope |
|-----|----------|-------|
| 1 | 1–4 | Morning Preparation: pre-prayer rituals, immersion, Akedah, synagogue entry |
| 2 | 5–11 | Shacharit through Ma'ariv: the full daily liturgical cycle, Amidah blessings |
| 3 | 12–15 | Shabbat: the weekly cycle, Partzuf-configurations, Shabbat meals |
| 4 | 16–19 | The Festivals: Rosh Hashanah, Yom Kippur, pilgrimage festivals |

## Key Cross-References
- Etz Chayyim Gates 19, 25, 44 (parallel kavvanot teachings within the Etz Chayyim)
- Rashash Siddur (Rabbi Shalom Sharabi, 1720–1777) — the most complete applied-kavvanot prayer book
- Pirkei d'Rabbi Eliezer ch. 45 (Rosh Chodesh for women)
- Sefer Yetzirah chs. 3–5 (letter-month-tribe correspondences)
