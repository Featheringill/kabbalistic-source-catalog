# Future Translation Candidates

Texts identified as priorities for future English translation. These have never been fully translated into English.

## Confirmed Candidates

### Tikune Teshuvah (Nathan of Gaza)
- **Location:** NYPL Ms. Heb. 185, fols. 40r–75r (35 folios)
- **Access:** [NYPL Digital Collections](https://digitalcollections.nypl.org/items/d0a11fc0-2602-0130-d287-58d385a7b928)
- **Status:** Never translated into English. Substantial text (35 folios, approximately 70 manuscript pages). Nathan of Gaza's penitential teaching — complements the Derush ha-Tanninim's cosmological framework.

### Additional Texts from NYPL Ms. Heb. 185
- 23 Sabbatean texts bound in a single codex, most never translated
- See `nypl-ms-heb-185/CONTENTS.md` for full catalog

## Tier 1: Frankist and Late-Sabbatian Primary Sources (Never Translated to English)

| Text | Author | Date | Language | Manuscript/Edition | Significance |
|------|--------|------|----------|-------------------|-------------|
| Divre Binah | Dov Ber Birkenthal | c. 1759-1800 | Hebrew | Autograph MS, Jerusalem | Eyewitness account of the 1759 Lvov disputation. Never translated into any modern language |
| Rozmaite adnotacyie (The Frankist Chronicle) | Frankist disciples | 1760s-1790s | Polish | Doktor critical edition 1996 | Narrative spine of the Frankist movement |
| Va-avo ha-Yom el ha-Ayin | Attrib. Jonathan Eibeschutz | Mid-18th c. | Hebrew | Critical edition in Be-Ikvot Mashiah 2021 | Most philosophically sophisticated late-Sabbatian treatise |
| Sefer ha-Beri'ah | Nathan of Gaza | c. 1665 | Hebrew | ~30 MSS, critical edition on Academia.edu | Natural sequel to Derush ha-Tanninim |
| Boker Avraham | Abraham Miguel Cardoso | Late 17th c. | Hebrew | Bodleian + many MSS | Most substantial Sabbatian systematic theology |
| Donme liturgical texts | Various | 18th-19th c. | Hebrew/Ladino/Turkish | Published by Scholem (Hebrew only) | A collected English "Donme Prayer Book" would be unprecedented |

## Tier 2: Pre-Lurianic Foundations (Planned)
- **Tikunei Zohar** — corrections/additions to the Zohar, traditionally attributed to Rabbi Shimon bar Yochai
- **Ma'arekhet ha-Elohut** — 13th-century systematic theology of the Sefirot
- **Sefer ha-Bahir** — earliest extant kabbalistic text (12th century), new critical edition

## Tier 3: Sabbatian-Frankist Sequence (Planned)
- **Nathan of Gaza** — additional works from manuscript (beyond Derush ha-Tanninim)
- **Jacob Frank / Slowa Panskie** — Harris Lenowitz translation exists (*The Collection of the Words of the Lord*, Archive.org); copyright held by Lenowitz estate (see note below). Focus is on identifying UNTRANSLATED Frankist texts.

## Copyright Note: Lenowitz Translation of Frank's Words of the Lord

- Harris Lenowitz (d. 2021, not 2013 as previously reported). Copyright extends to 2091.
- The Internet Archive upload (2009) carries an erroneous "Public Domain" designation — uploaded by a third party, not by Lenowitz or his estate.
- Copyright held by Lenowitz's personal estate, not the University of Utah.
- Contact routes for permission: (1) University of Utah Dept. of World Languages & Cultures, (2) Jerome Rothenberg, UC San Diego (Lenowitz's close collaborator, wrote 2021 memorial).
- Alternative path: Independent translation from Doktor's 1997 Polish critical edition of the original Polish manuscripts (public domain source texts).

## Research Notes
- Lenowitz translation covers Jacob Frank's sayings from Polish manuscripts — NOT Nathan of Gaza
- Any future Frankist work must resolve copyright status before proceeding
- Additional Sabbatean manuscripts may exist at JTS (Adler collection), National Library of Israel, Ben-Zvi Institute, and Bodleian Library Oxford
