## Scholarly Significance
This codex is one of the most important single collections of Sabbatean primary texts. It was among the sources used by Gershom Scholem for his critical editions of Nathan of Gaza's works. The codex's 1697 Jerusalem provenance indicates continued Sabbatean activity in the Land of Israel well after Sabbatai Zevi's death (1676) and Nathan of Gaza's death (1680).
