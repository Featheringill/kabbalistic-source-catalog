# Emek HaMelech — Source Catalog

- **Author:** Rabbi Naftali Hertz Bachrach (fl. first half of 17th century)
- **Edition used:** Amsterdam 1648 (first and only early edition)
- **Printer:** Emanuel Benveniste, Amsterdam
- **Digital source:** [hebrewbooks.org/14359](https://hebrewbooks.org/14359)
- **Copyright status:** Public domain
- **Pages:** 403

## About the Work
The Emek HaMelech is an independent systematization of Lurianic Kabbalah from the Israel Sarug school, which diverges from Vital's Etz Chayyim on several critical points — notably the nature of the Tehiru Ila'ah (upper brightness) and the pre-Tzimtzum state of the divine light. The Sarug-Bachrach school represents an alternative Lurianic transmission brought to Italy and Western Europe.

## The Sarug-Vital Divergence
- **Vital school:** Tehiru Ila'ah = the post-Tzimtzum void; evil enters through the Shevirah
- **Sarug-Bachrach school:** Tehiru Ila'ah = the pre-Tzimtzum state of the Or Ein Sof; evil = ontological distance from pre-contraction luminosity

## Structure (12 Sha'arim across 3 volumes)

| Vol | Sections | Scope |
|-----|----------|-------|
| 1 | Nefesh David, Sha'ashu'ei ha-Melekh, Or Qadmon | Cosmological foundation: pre-Tzimtzum doctrine |
| 2 | Adam Kadmon through Kiryat Arba (7 sha'arim) | Structure of the worlds and Partzufim |
| 3 | Reisha di-Ze'ir Anpin, Olam ha-Beriyah | Head of Ze'ir Anpin and the World of Creation |

## Key Features
- Uses topical sub-gate names (שער + topic) rather than numbered gates beyond Gates 1–2
- Uses פרק as short paragraph-units (~5–15 lines), not formal chapters
- 12 topical she'arim + Nefesh David prolegomenon
- OCLC-registered author name: "Bachrach, Naftali Hertz" (Hertz = Tzvi, both meaning "deer")

## Historical Context
Published in Amsterdam in 1648 — the year of the Chmielnicki massacres in Eastern Europe. Whether Bachrach was directly affected is undocumented.

## Cross-References
- Etz Chayyim Gates 1–9 (parallel cosmological teachings from the Vital school)
- Sefer Yetzirah (pre-kabbalistic cosmological framework)
- Nathan of Gaza, Derush ha-Tanninim (1665) — departs from both Vital and Bachrach at the Tehiru
