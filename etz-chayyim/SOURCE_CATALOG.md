## About the Work
The Etz Chayyim is the foundational text of Lurianic Kabbalah — the most influential school of Jewish mysticism from the 16th century onward. It presents the complete cosmological system of Rabbi Isaac Luria ("the Ari") as recorded by his foremost disciple, Rabbi Chayyim Vital. The system begins with the Tzimtzum (divine contraction), proceeds through the Shevirat ha-Kelim (Breaking of the Vessels), and culminates in the Tikkun (cosmic repair) that is the ongoing purpose of creation.

## Seven-Palace Architecture

The fifty gates organize into seven thematic palaces:
1. **Palace of Foundations** (Gates 1–8): Tzimtzum through Shevirah
2. **Palace of the Partzufim** (Gates 9–14): The divine configurations
3. **Palace of Practice** (Gates 15–30): Souls, worlds, prayer, speech, thought
4. **Palace of the Virtues** (Gates 31–34): Love, fear, repentance, holiness
5. **Palace of Advanced Practice** (Gates 35–44): Faith through deep kavvanot
6. **Palace of Eschatology** (Gates 45–49): Tikkun, sparks, Messiah, Adam Kadmon
7. **The Fiftieth Gate** (Gate 50): The culmination
