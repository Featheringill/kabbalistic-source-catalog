## T1 Standard

All five works have been upgraded to the T1 commentary standard:

- **Commentary boxes** use plain-text labels ([DOCTRINAL NOTE], [SCHOLARLY NOTE], [TEXTUAL-CRITICAL NOTE], [VOICE NOTE]) instead of Unicode symbols
- **Section dividers** standardized to ✦ ✦ ✦
- **Box borders** differentiated by line style (solid, grey, dashed, dotted) for print accessibility
- **No legacy Unicode symbols** (◈, ⬡, ⚑, ▶, ◐) remain in any volume

## Series Arc

The five completed works trace the full arc of Lurianic Kabbalah from its systematic foundation through its most radical departure:

1. **Etz Chayyim** (Vital/Luria) — the foundational system
2. **Sha'ar ha-Kavanot** (Vital) — the liturgical application
3. **Sha'ar ha-Gilgulim** (Vital) — the doctrine of soul-transmigration
4. **Emek HaMelech** (Bachrach/Sarug) — the divergent school (Tehiru Ila'ah dispute)
5. **Derush ha-Tanninim** (Nathan of Gaza) — the Sabbatian theological rupture

Together these constitute the **first complete Lurianic-to-Sabbatian doctrinal library in any Western language**.
