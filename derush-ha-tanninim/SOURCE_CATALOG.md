## MANDATORY DISAMBIGUATION
Two distinct works bear similar titles:
1. **This text:** Nathan of Gaza's 1665 Sabbatian cosmological treatise on the Tehiru, the Thought-less Lights, and the Tanninim (dragons) within the primordial void
2. **NOT this text:** R. Yosef ibn Tabul's (d. c. 1610) pre-Sabbatean Lurianic treatise on related cosmological questions

The two share terminology but represent different schools, different historical moments, and incompatible theological conclusions.

## About the Work
The founding theological document of Sabbatianism. Nathan of Gaza provides a radical reinterpretation of Lurianic cosmology that places the Messiah's soul-root within the realm of the kelipot (shells of evil), thereby providing theological infrastructure for antinomian messianic claims. No prior complete scholarly English translation exists.
